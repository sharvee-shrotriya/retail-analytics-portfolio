# Methodology
## Costco Supply Chain Restructuring — 2026

---

### Research Question
*How exposed is Costco Wholesale to tariff escalation across its key trade partners (China, Mexico, Canada), and how effective are their current restructuring strategies at protecting gross margin?*

---

### Analytical Approach

#### 1. Framing the Problem (Before Touching Data)
Before pulling any data, I defined the audience and decision context: a supply chain & procurement leadership team that needs to know *what action to take*, not just what the numbers say. This shaped every choice downstream — what to measure, how to present it, what to leave out.

#### 2. Sourcing Mix Estimation
Costco does not publicly disclose exact import origin percentages. I triangulated from three sources:
- U.S. Census Bureau USA Trade Online (import volumes by HTS code and origin country)
- Costco 10-K segment disclosures (product category revenue mix)
- Earnings call commentary (CFO statements on China exposure and Vietnam diversification)

The result is an estimated origin mix, clearly labeled as such in the dashboard. Precision matters less than directional accuracy for strategic decisions.

#### 3. Tariff Impact Modeling
Gross margin impact was modeled by:
1. Identifying Costco's highest-tariff-exposed segment (general merchandise / hardlines, ~35% of revenue)
2. Applying effective tariff rates from CBP schedules to estimated import mix
3. Calculating landed cost increase as a % of category revenue
4. Converting to gross margin basis points using COST's disclosed segment margins

Each mitigation scenario was modeled independently, then stacked to show cumulative recovery potential.

#### 4. Scenario Development
Four mitigation scenarios were built:
- **Inventory Pre-Buy:** Modeled on disclosed stockpiling activity and estimated sell-through runway
- **Vendor Negotiation:** Benchmarked against industry reports on supplier concession capacity
- **Vietnam/India Dual Sourcing:** Based on CFO comments and Vietnam tariff rate differentials
- **Full Mexico Nearshore:** Modeled at 40% category shift with USMCA landed cost assumptions

#### 5. AI Adoption Benchmarking
Industry-level AI adoption data (Deloitte, Amperity) used as a proxy for where Costco sits relative to peers. Costco's narrow SKU count was identified as a structural enabler for AI-driven inventory tools — a qualitative insight layered onto the quantitative benchmarks.

---

### What I Deliberately Left Out
- Stock price analysis (not relevant for a procurement audience)
- Consumer sentiment data (a separate workstream)
- Competitor deep-dives (would dilute focus — separate projects planned)

---

### Limitations
- Sourcing mix is estimated, not disclosed
- Margin impact figures are illustrative models
- Geopolitical scenarios (tariff de-escalation) are directional only
- Data reflects publicly available information as of Q1 2026

---

*This methodology reflects how I approach analytical projects: define the decision first, then build backward to the data.*
