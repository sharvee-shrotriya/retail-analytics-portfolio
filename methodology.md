# Methodology
### The Kirkland Moat — Costco Supply Chain Analysis

---

## Research Approach

This analysis follows a primary-source-first methodology. Every data point was sourced directly from SEC filings, earnings transcripts, court records, or named trade press — not aggregated databases or secondary summaries.

The analytical framework is structured around a single research question: does Kirkland Signature's local sourcing model create durable competitive advantage beyond tariff avoidance? All data collection, framing, and scenario modeling flows from that question.

---

## Process

**Step 1 — Identify the business event**
The trigger was Costco's Q3 FY2025 earnings miss. EPS of $4.02 vs. $4.11 estimate. Stock down 7% in one session. Supply chain costs cited. The CEO's comments on Kirkland's sourcing pivot in the same call signaled a deeper strategic story worth investigating.

**Step 2 — Define the audience and decision context**
This analysis is written for operations, strategy, and business intelligence professionals evaluating supply chain resilience and private label strategy. The question is not whether Costco will survive tariffs — it is whether the sourcing model holds structural value independent of any policy outcome.

**Step 3 — Collect data from primary sources**
All data was pulled directly from Costco's 10-K (SEC EDGAR), the Q3 FY2025 earnings call transcript, Court of International Trade filings, and named competitor disclosures. See data-sources.md for the full list.

**Step 4 — Separate signal from noise**
Not all data points from the earnings call or 10-K are relevant to the research question. Data selected for the dashboard met two criteria: it directly addressed sourcing strategy, cost structure, or competitive positioning, and it was verifiable from the primary source rather than inferred.

**Step 5 — Build the competitive comparison**
Member's Mark (Sam's Club) was chosen as the primary benchmark because it is the closest structural analog to Kirkland — a warehouse club private label competing on quality and value. Walmart Great Value and Target owned brands were included as secondary reference points for China sourcing exposure.

**Step 6 — Stress-test the thesis**
The scenario analysis (145% / 15% / 0% tariff) was built to test whether the core thesis — local sourcing wins regardless of tariff outcome — holds under adverse, current, and favorable policy conditions. The goal was to find a scenario where Kirkland's advantage disappears. No such scenario was found within realistic parameters.

**Step 7 — Frame the so-what**
Analytical findings are translated into strategic implications for three audiences: Costco as an operator, Sam's Club as a competitor, and analysts modeling private label supply chains. The framing distinguishes between a structural moat and a policy hedge.

---

## Analytical Principles

**Distinguish structural from cyclical.** A supply chain advantage that only works in one policy environment is a bet, not a moat. This analysis specifically tests whether Kirkland's advantage persists across regimes.

**Prioritize primary sources.** Earnings transcripts and 10-K filings take precedence over aggregated research or secondary news coverage. Where secondary sources are used, they are named and noted in data-sources.md.

**Rate data extraction difficulty upfront.** All data sources were evaluated for accessibility before analysis began. This project used only Easy-rated sources — no restricted APIs, paywalled databases, or scraping.

**Write for decision-makers, not data consumers.** The dashboard is structured around the research question, not the data. Charts and tables serve the argument; they are not the argument.
