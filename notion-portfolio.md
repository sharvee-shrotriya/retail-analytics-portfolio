# Project 01
## Costco Supply Chain Restructuring — 2026
**[View Live Dashboard →](#)** *(add GitHub Pages URL)*

**Category:** Supply Chain · Procurement Intelligence · Retail
**Audience:** Supply chain & procurement leadership
**Published:** March 2026

---

### The Question
Costco missed Q3 FY2025 earnings. CFO Gary Millerchip cited higher supply chain costs. The stock dropped 7% in a single day.

Everyone reported the headline. I wanted to understand the structure underneath it.

*How exposed is Costco to tariff escalation — and is their restructuring strategy actually enough to protect margin?*

---

### What I Built
A single-page procurement intelligence brief covering:

- **Sourcing exposure by trade partner** — China, Mexico, Canada, domestic, and emerging alternatives
- **Tariff scenario modeling** — four mitigation paths with estimated gross margin impact
- **Procurement response tracker** — what Costco is actually doing, and what's risky
- **Margin waterfall** — basis-point bridge from 2025 baseline to 2026 forecast
- **AI adoption benchmarking** — where retail industry is heading on supply chain tech

---

### Key Findings

**1. The exposure is concentrated, not diversified**
~58% of hardlines and general merchandise sourced from China at a 145% effective tariff rate. That's not a headwind — it's a structural cost problem.

**2. Costco's real advantage is SKU count, not buying power**
~4,000 SKUs per warehouse vs. Walmart's 140,000+. Sourcing pivots that take Walmart 18+ months take Costco 6–9. That's a genuine procurement edge.

**3. The Kirkland domestic shift is the smartest move**
70% of Kirkland Signature production moved to U.S. manufacturers. This permanently removes a cost variable — not just a tariff workaround.

**4. One strategy carries hidden geopolitical risk**
Pressuring Chinese suppliers to absorb the tariff delta. Beijing publicly pushed back on Walmart for the same tactic. Costco needs an exit plan.

**5. Membership fees are a buffer — but a finite one**
At 65% of net profit from fees, Costco can absorb ~120–150bp of margin compression before the value proposition to members is at risk. Current estimated exposure: ~380bp from China tariffs alone.

### Methodology Snapshot
1. Defined the audience and decision context *before* touching data
2. Triangulated sourcing mix from Census data + 10-K disclosures + earnings commentary
3. Modeled tariff impact on general merchandise segment (~35% of COST revenue)
4. Built four independent mitigation scenarios, stacked to show cumulative recovery
5. Layered qualitative insight (SKU count advantage, geopolitical risk) onto quantitative model
